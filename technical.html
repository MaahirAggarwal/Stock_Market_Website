Introduction to Technical Analysis

Technical analysis is the art and science of forecasting future prices based on the examination of past price movements. It is built on key assumptions:

  • Price Discounts Everything: All relevant information is already reflected in the stock price.
  • Price Movements Are Not Random: Prices move in identifiable trends.
  • History Tends to Repeat Itself: Market psychology drives repetitive patterns over time.

Candlestick Patterns

Hammer: Occurs in a downtrend when bulls make a start to step into the rally. The lower shadow is at least twice the length of the body. This is a bullish reversal pattern, indicating the bottoming out of a trend

Shooting Star: A bearish reversal pattern that occurs after an uptrend, signaling rejection of higher prices. The long upper shadow shows selling pressure at higher levels.

Additional patterns like Engulfing Patterns, Harami Patterns, and Doji Candles provide insights into market sentiment

Important Chart Patterns

Chart patterns provide a graphical representation of market psychology. Here are some key patterns:

  • Head and Shoulders: A reversal pattern that occurs after an extended uptrend. The middle peak (head) is higher than the two outside peaks (shoulders)
  • Double Top and Double Bottom: A double top indicates a bearish reversal, while a double bottom suggests bullish reversal
  • Triangles: Symmetrical, ascending, and descending triangles indicate periods of consolidation before a breakout

Support & Resistance

Support: A price level where demand is strong enough to stop a price from falling further.

Resistance: A price level where selling pressure is enough to stop a price from rising further

These levels are not exact points but zones where market psychology shifts. Support can turn into resistance and vice versa .
